Album Cover Design


For this project I was able to select the artist and music. I felt this album deserved a personal connection to reality, so I wanted to capture my design in an image rather than making it digitally. The melancholy tone reflects the title and related song. It is a cry for help. The water droplets reflects the word cry allowing them to become tears when the viewer translates the image in their mind.

To create this image I used water droplets on plastic over a pan and a paint brush. It took many hours to manipulate the water into the forms and letters, but worked out very effectively. Next I put the image in photoshop and brought out the blues and grays to really set that mood. On the back I muted the same design to keep the cover unified while also giving the text on the back readability.

I am very happy with how this design turned out and I feel like it perfectly captures the album and song.



App & Icon Design


For this project the goal was to design an app to help people identify venomous animals in Missouri. I made the logo out of snake fangs into a “v” for venomous to interconnect this ideology. The really fun part of designing this however, was making the icons.

They went through multiple redesigns as I wanted to identify their recognizable features, while also making it simple and easier to remember. I landed on a design that used very little detail and few colors. Having all of them include black and sometimes blue to interconnect them with the apps logo and background interface.

This design is very successful in meeting the desired outcome from its conception. Its simple, helpful, and easy to remember.

Book Cover Design


This project’s goal was to create a cover that accurately represented the contents of the book. The book is the urban history of Springfield Missouri. With this in mind, and my experience growing up there I knew exactly what to do for the design.

Naturally I included Missouri with a star showing Springfield’s location inside of it. That star reflects the other major icon in the design which along with the color scheme comes from the Springfield city flag. Lastly I incorporated a skyline that shows the history and diversity of the city. It includes famous buildings, airports, a train, some urban residences, and a farm. This cover perfectly encapsulates Springfield, the contents of the book, and draws the reader in to learn more about the history of the city.
